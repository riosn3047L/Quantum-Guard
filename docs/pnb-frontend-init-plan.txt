## PROJECT OVERVIEW
QuantumGuard is an open-source cybersecurity maturity assessment platform that helps organizations evaluate their quantum readiness. It assesses vulnerability to quantum computing threats across 4 dimensions, 12 practices, and 120 questions, then provides scoring, maturity levels, compliance mapping, gap analysis, and executive dashboards.

#### Quick Assessment Questions (12 questions):

**Dimension 1: CVI (Cryptographic Visibility & Inventory)**
Q1: "How does your organization identify and catalog cryptographic assets (algorithms, keys, certificates) across your infrastructure?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "No systematic identification; cryptographic assets are unknown"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Partial manual inventory exists for some critical systems"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Automated discovery tools scan most systems with a maintained inventory"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Comprehensive real-time CBOM with automated discovery across all assets"

Q2: "How does your organization assess which cryptographic implementations are vulnerable to quantum attacks?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "No quantum vulnerability assessment has been performed"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Basic awareness of quantum-vulnerable algorithms (RSA, ECC, DH)"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Formal assessment completed with prioritized vulnerability list"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Continuous automated scanning with risk-rated quantum vulnerability tracking"

Q3: "How well does your organization understand cryptographic dependencies in third-party software and supply chain?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "No visibility into third-party cryptographic dependencies"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Some awareness of major vendor cryptographic implementations"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Systematic vendor questionnaire process with cryptographic requirements"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Automated supply chain cryptographic analysis with CBOM integration"

**Dimension 2: SGRM (Strategic Governance & Risk Management)**
Q4: "Does your organization have executive-level sponsorship and a formal strategy for post-quantum cryptography migration?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "No executive awareness or strategy for quantum threats"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Executive awareness exists but no formal strategy or budget"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Formal PQC migration strategy with executive sponsorship and dedicated budget"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Board-level governance with integrated quantum risk in enterprise risk management"

Q5: "How does your organization assess and manage quantum-related risks in compliance and regulatory contexts?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "Quantum risks are not included in risk assessments"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Quantum risks acknowledged but not formally assessed"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Quantum risks integrated into formal risk assessment framework"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Proactive compliance monitoring with quantum-specific regulatory tracking"

Q6: "How does your organization manage quantum readiness of third-party vendors and supply chain partners?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "No vendor quantum readiness requirements exist"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Informal discussions with key vendors about PQC plans"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Formal vendor assessment questionnaire includes quantum readiness criteria"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Contractual PQC requirements with verified vendor migration timelines"

**Dimension 3: DPE (Data Protection Engineering)**
Q7: "How does your organization classify data based on quantum risk exposure (e.g., long-lived sensitive data)?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "No data classification considers quantum risk or data lifespan"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Basic awareness that some data has long-term sensitivity"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Formal classification scheme includes quantum risk based on data lifespan and sensitivity"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Dynamic classification with automated harvest-now-decrypt-later risk scoring"

Q8: "What is the status of your organization's encryption at rest with respect to quantum-safe algorithms?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "Using only classical encryption with no PQC considerations"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Evaluating PQC algorithms for storage encryption"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Pilot implementations of hybrid or PQC encryption for critical data stores"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Production deployment of quantum-safe encryption for all sensitive data at rest"

Q9: "How prepared are your network protocols and data-in-transit protections for quantum threats?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "Standard TLS/IPsec with no quantum-safe considerations"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Awareness of quantum-vulnerable cipher suites; initial TLS assessment done"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Testing hybrid TLS configurations with PQC key exchange in non-production"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Production hybrid or PQC-only TLS deployed with CNSA 2.0 compliance"

**Dimension 4: ITR (Implementation & Technical Readiness)**
Q10: "How well has your organization assessed infrastructure readiness (performance, compatibility) for PQC algorithms?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "No infrastructure assessment for PQC has been conducted"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Basic research on PQC algorithm performance characteristics"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Formal benchmarking of PQC algorithms on representative infrastructure"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Comprehensive infrastructure readiness with capacity planning for PQC deployment"

Q11: "What is your organization's capability to implement and deploy post-quantum cryptographic solutions?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "No PQC implementation experience or capability"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Team has basic PQC knowledge; exploring available libraries"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Successful pilot implementations with trained team and documented procedures"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Production-ready implementation capability with crypto-agile architecture"

Q12: "How does your organization test and validate post-quantum cryptographic implementations?"
  - Option 1 (Score 1): "No PQC testing or validation processes exist"
  - Option 2 (Score 2): "Ad-hoc testing of PQC libraries in isolated environments"
  - Option 3 (Score 3): "Formal test plans with interoperability, performance, and security validation"
  - Option 4 (Score 4): "Automated CI/CD PQC testing pipeline with continuous validation and compliance checks"

### PAGE 4: Organization Profile (Route: /assessment/profile)
- Form to capture organization context BEFORE starting assessment
- Fields:
  1. Organization Name (text input)
  2. Industry (dropdown): Financial Services, Healthcare, Government/Defense, Technology, Energy/Utilities, Telecommunications, Manufacturing, Education, Retail, Other
  3. Organizational Size (radio): Small (<500), Medium (500-5000), Large (5000-50000), Enterprise (50000+)

  5. Data Sensitivity Level (radio): Low, Medium, High, Critical
  6. Technology Complexity (radio): Basic, Moderate, Complex
- Auto-calculate Profile Multiplier (0.8x - 1.5x) shown in real-time:
  - Industry: Financial/Gov/Defense = 1.5, Healthcare/Energy = 1.3, Tech/Telecom = 1.1, Others = 0.8-1.0
  - Regulatory: 4+ frameworks = 1.5, 2-3 = 1.2, 1 = 1.0, none = 0.8
  - Scale: Enterprise = 1.3, Large = 1.2, Medium = 1.0, Small = 0.8
  - Sensitivity: Critical = 1.5, High = 1.3, Medium = 1.0, Low = 0.8
  - Complexity: Complex = 1.3, Moderate = 1.0, Basic = 0.8
  - Final Multiplier = weighted average (Industry 25%, Regulatory 25%, Scale 20%, Sensitivity 20%, Complexity 10%)
